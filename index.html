Our Story

From one coach to a movement of fifty

The Empower Project began in January 2021 with a single staff member and a bold vision: that every person — regardless of ability, background, or circumstance — deserves the chance to lead a meaningful life and rewarding career.

Today we've grown to 50 employees supporting over 300 individuals across 12 metro area counties. We're accredited by the Commission on Accreditation of Rehabilitation Facilities (CARF), hold a 245D license in multiple categories, and were named "Community Transition Provider of the Year" by the MN Rehabilitation Association in 2023.

Programs & Services

Tailored Support for Every Step

A continuum of services designed to meet you where you are and guide you toward where you want to be.

🧭

Career Exploration & Coaching

One-on-one career guidance including resume building, interview preparation, job placement, and ongoing workplace mentorship through our employer partnerships.

🎓

Education & Training Prep

Post-secondary education preparation, IT certification pathways, cashier training, and art-based skill development — all tailored to individual goals and interests.

💼

Workplace Readiness

Pre-employment skills training, 8–12 week paid work experiences with partner companies, and on-the-job support that often leads to permanent employment.

🏡

Independent Living Skills

Practical workshops on financial literacy, daily living, communication, self-advocacy, and community navigation — personalized plans that meet you exactly where you are.

🎲

Social Skills & Community

Creative engagement opportunities including our Dungeons & Dragons social skills group, community workshops, and inclusive activities designed to build confidence and connection.

🏢

Employer Training & Consulting

We help businesses develop inclusive hiring programs, attract diverse talent, and build workplace cultures where every team member can contribute and thrive.

Our Vision

A future where everyone who wants to work finds meaningful employment

The Empower Project was created to serve a wider range of individuals — not just those eligible for traditional VRS services — and open new doors to skills, experience, and lasting employment.

  • Every individual finds competitive, meaningful employment regardless of background
  • Employers recognize and value the diverse talents they might otherwise overlook
  • Individuals achieve self-sufficiency and become active, contributing community members
  • Communities see the benefit of investing in every person's potential
